I. Chapter One · Spring

The thaw, and then everything at once.

Ice breaks up on the lake. Garden centers open. The Farmers Market sets its tents up on the square for the first time, and half the town shows up just to say hello.

Spring in Beaver Dam is a gentle, neighborly affair — sap running, trails muddy, patio chairs coming out of sheds. It is the best time to introduce yourself.

II. Chapter Two · Summer

Loud, golden, slightly buttery.

By June, the lake is warm enough. By July, fireworks. By August, the whole town smells, pleasantly, of sweet corn. Summer is when Beaver Dam puts on its best show.

Taste of Wisconsin, the Corn Roast, wine tours, live music downtown — the Chamber calendar more or less writes itself. Show up. Order a little of everything.

III. Chapter Three · Autumn

Sweater weather on the lake.

The trees put on one of the best shows in southeastern Wisconsin. Sunset runs long and slow. You can almost hear the town exhale.

Autumn is pepper festivals and apple stands, Friday-night lights and early dinners, and the season when most visitors go "oh. I could live here." More than a few have.

IV. Chapter Four · Winter

Quiet streets, bright windows.

Downtown lights up in December. Ice fishermen dot the lake by January. The fish fry lines get a little longer. The town turns in on itself, in the best possible way.

Come for the holiday parade, stay for the supper club. Winter in Beaver Dam is slower and warmer than it sounds — and the Chamber calendar keeps the lights on all season long.
