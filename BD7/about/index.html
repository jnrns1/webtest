ABOUT THE CHAMBER & VISITOR CENTER

Your community connection on the lake.

Since 1972 we've been the seat at the table for Beaver Dam businesses and the front door for visitors and new residents. One organization, two roles — both built on the same idea: a small Wisconsin city is a better small Wisconsin city when its people know each other.

OUR MISSION

An engaging community with prosperous businesses.

That's the line we've held for fifty-plus years, and it still does the work. Visitors find a real welcome. Residents find their neighbors. Businesses find a network that takes the long view.

We host the events, run the Visitor Center, advocate at city hall, train the next generation through Leadership Beaver Dam and the Young Professional Network, and keep the printed Community Guide in 30,000 hands every year.

WHAT WE DO

One organization, four daily jobs.

The Chamber wears more hats than its size suggests. Here's the day-to-day.

VISITOR CENTER

The front door for tourists, families, and newcomers.

Maps, the Community Guide, lake-day tips, and a friendly hello. Open Mon–Fri at 127 S. Spring St.

MEMBER NETWORK

Mixers, ribbon cuttings, referrals — for 400+ local businesses.

Networking that turns into real word-of-mouth. Marketing that puts you in the Community Guide. Advocacy at city hall and the county.

EVENTS

87 events a year that bring the town together.

From the Taste of Wisconsin to the Beaver Bar Crawl to the Annual Awards Gala.

PROGRAMS

Three flagship programs that build the next generation of Beaver Dam.

Leadership Beaver Dam — a 9-month immersion. The Young Professional Network — under-40 leaders. The Manufacturing Business Alliance — peer council for plant managers.
