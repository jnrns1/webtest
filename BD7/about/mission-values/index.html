MISSION & VALUES

Why we keep the lights on.

A small Wisconsin city is a better small Wisconsin city when its people know each other. Everything we do — the events, the directory, the printed Community Guide, the volunteer hours — comes back to that one idea.

OUR MISSION

An engaging community with prosperous businesses.

That's the line we've held for fifty-plus years and it still does the work. Visitors find a real welcome. Residents find their neighbors. Businesses find a network that takes the long view. We are the seat at the table — and the front door — for Beaver Dam, Wisconsin.

OUR VISION

A lake town that punches above its weight.

Beaver Dam is a city you can settle into without giving up nature, downtown, or the weekend. Our vision is to keep it that way — and to keep adding reasons it's worth the drive.

VALUE · 01

Show up.

Ribbon cuttings. Funerals. Grand openings. Every Friday-night concert in the park. Presence is the work.

VALUE · 02

Make introductions.

The new restaurant should know the bank. The plant manager should know the school superintendent. We close that loop.

VALUE · 03

Invest in the next generation.

Leadership Beaver Dam, the Young Professional Network, Youth Apprenticeship — the chamber thirty years from now starts in school.

VALUE · 04 — THE LONG VIEW

Plan in decades, not quarters.

Most of our flagship events are older than this paragraph. The Corn Roast is in its 63rd year. The Golf Outing is in its 32nd. Things last because we don't reinvent them every May.

VALUE · 05

Keep the building open.

A real visitor center with a real person inside, Monday through Friday. Maps, recommendations, a printed guide. Not a chatbot.

VALUE · 06

Stay neutral. Stay useful.

We advocate for our members at city hall and the county. We don't pick winners. The directory lists every member equally; the calendar serves every member's events.

HOW WE MEASURE IT

A mission is only real if you can count it.

We report the numbers to our board, our members, and anyone who walks in and asks. Here's what last year looked like in plain digits.

412+ member businesses A net-positive year. 38 new members; 14 new ribbon cuttings.
87 events on the calendar Free, ticketed, member-only, and city-wide combined.
30,000 Community Guides Distributed to visitors, hotels, real-estate offices, and newcomer welcome bags.
2,400+ ambassador hours Volunteer time donated by members at events, ribbon cuttings, and the visitor center.
