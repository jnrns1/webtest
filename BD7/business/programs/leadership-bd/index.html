Class of 2026 applications open

9 months. 24 seats. One city, inside out.

Leadership Beaver Dam is the chamber's flagship leadership immersion. Once a month, for nine months, your cohort spends a full day inside the institutions that run our city — city hall, the hospital, the schools, manufacturing, courts, nonprofits, and the lake. You'll graduate knowing the town the way the people who built it know it.

CURRICULUM

9 days. 9 sectors. The whole city.

Every session runs 7:30am–4:30pm at the host institution, with lunch on-site. Attendance is required at 8 of 9 — graduation depends on it.

SEPT

Orientation & History

Beaver Dam's founding, its industries, the lake, and the chamber's first 50 years.

OCT

City Government

Mayor's office, common council, public works, parks & rec, planning.

NOV

Public Safety & Justice

Police, fire, EMS, the courthouse, and the county jail.

DEC

Education K–College

BDUSD elementary, middle, and high; Moraine Park Tech.

JAN

Health & Human Services

Marshfield Clinic, hospice, food pantry, community action.

FEB

Manufacturing

Behind the scenes at three Dodge County manufacturers.

MAR

Agriculture & the Lake

Farms, dairy, and the Beaver Dam Lake watershed.

APR

Quality of Life

Arts, parks, downtown, and the volunteer sector.

MAY

Capstone & Graduation

Cohort presents its capstone projects — community-impact proposals built across the year. Graduation reception with the board, alumni, and host partners.
