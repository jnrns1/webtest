DOWNTOWN BEAVER DAM

The square. The shops. The gazebo.

A real downtown. The kind where you bump into your neighbor at the coffee shop, browse the bookstore on a Saturday, and end the evening on the patio of a Wisconsin supper club. Twelve walkable blocks, anchored by the gazebo on Park Avenue.

A WALKABLE MAIN STREET

A Saturday-morning kind of downtown.

From the Visitor Center on Spring Street, the whole district is yours in twelve unhurried blocks. Cafés, a brewery, a bookshop, three good restaurants, a community theatre, the farmers market, and the gazebo always staffed by something — a band, a bake sale, a wedding photo.

Shops Boutiques, antiques, books, gifts, and a hardware store that still cuts keys.
Eat & drink Supper clubs, craft brewers, cafés, and a Friday fish fry.
Stages Community theatre, gazebo concerts, public art on the town.

WHAT'S DOWNTOWN

Six reasons to leave the car at home.

Browse a few of the chamber-member spots that make downtown what it is. The Member Directory has the full list — including hours, menus, and contacts.

ART ON THE TOWN

Public art, galleries, and a downtown that takes design seriously.

The annual Art on the Town festival turns Park Avenue into a gallery for a weekend — and leaves a few new sculptures behind every year.
COMMUNITY THEATRE

A 90-year-old theatre with a six-show season.

The Beaver Dam Area Community Theatre runs year-round — musicals, dramas, and the holiday show that everyone goes to.

 CAFÉS & BAKERIES

Coffee, scones, and the table by the window.

A handful of independents within four blocks of each other. You'll find your spot.
FRIDAYS & SATURDAYS

Live music, the farmers market, and the gazebo.

Saturday mornings the market spills onto Park Avenue. Friday nights, the gazebo is a stage. Bring a chair, a kid, and a sweater.

SUPPER CLUBS & BREWERIES

Wisconsin classics — brandy old fashioneds, fish fries, prime rib.

One brewery in town and a few more within twenty minutes. The brandy old fashioned is a religion.

 SHOPPING

Boutiques, antiques, gifts, and a real hardware store.

Independently owned shops you'll actually want to browse. Park once, walk all of it.

 DOWNTOWN LIVING

Lofts and second-floor apartments above the shops.

A small but growing inventory of downtown rentals — for empty-nesters, transplants, and anyone tired of mowing.
FIND DOWNTOWN

Where to park. Where to start.

Free public parking around Park Avenue and on Center Street. The Visitor Center at 127 S. Spring Street is the easiest place to start — pick up a printed map, grab a coffee recommendation, then walk.
