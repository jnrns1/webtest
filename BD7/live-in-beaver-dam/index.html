Home/Live in Beaver Dam

PUT DOWN ROOTS

Stay a weekend. Or a lifetime.

Beaver Dam is a city you can settle into without giving up your nature, your downtown, or your weekends. Affordable homes, schools that punch above their weight, twelve city parks, and a lake you can see from Spring Street.

WHY MOVE HERE

A small Wisconsin city that still feels like one.

Population around 15,000 — big enough for everything you need, small enough that the barista knows your kid's name. Two hours from Milwaukee and Madison, twenty minutes from Highway 151, a Friday-fish-fry tradition that goes back generations.

Affordable Median home price well below the WI average.
Walkable Downtown to the lake in twelve minutes on foot.
Connected 120 mins to MKE/MSN. 25 mins to Highway 151.

A snapshot of the day-to-day — from the lake at dawn to the gazebo on a summer Friday.

THE LAKE

Sunsets, fishing, and a public boat launch four blocks from downtown.

Beaver Dam Lake anchors the city. Kayaking before work, fishing on weekends, sunset on the dock most nights of the year.
EVENTS & FESTIVALS

87 events a year — most of them free, all of them welcoming.

Concerts in the Park, the Pepper Festival, the Bar Crawl, fireworks on the Fourth. The calendar is the heartbeat of living here.

 SCHOOLS

Beaver Dam Unified School District — K through 12.

Public schools that hold their own. Private & parochial options too. Families move here for the schools as often as they move for the lake.

DOWNTOWN

Walkable shops, cafés, and the gazebo on the square.

Saturday-morning kind of downtown. Cafés, a brewery, a bookshop, a few good restaurants, and the gazebo always staffed by a bake-sale.
